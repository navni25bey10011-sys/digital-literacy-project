CASE STUDY ON PHISHING

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate trusted organizations to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. It usually begins with a fake email, message, or website that appears legitimate.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email claiming to be from a bank or an online shopping platform. The message often creates urgency, such as warning about account suspension or suspicious activity. The email contains a link that redirects the user to a fake website designed tolook identical to the original one. When the user enters their login credentials or financial details, the attacker captures this information.

Students, working professionals, and elderly people are common targets because they may not always verify the authenticity of such messages. Once the attacker gains access to the victim’s information, they can perform unauthorized transactions, steal money, or misuse personal data.

The consequences of phishing attacks include financial loss, identity theft, and emotional stress. This highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting online and verifying sources before sharing any sensitive information.
